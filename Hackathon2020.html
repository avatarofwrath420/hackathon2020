What is Covid-19?

Covid-19, or the Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome-Related Coronavirus 2, is like most viruses:

It is capable of reproducing itself only though the entering of a host cell. It's makep consists of a lipid
shell dotted with protein spikes, holding its genetic material within. However, unlike its SARS counterpart, its spikes are 10 times more powerful, leading to a greater rate of transmission.

The Coronavirus spreads mainly through respiratory droplets, tiny particles of body fluid that have been aerosolized via sneezing or coughing. Surfaces stained with the virus take up to 3-4 days to sterilize.

Droplets exhaled at great force during exercise or vocal performace may spread further than 6 feet. The coronavirus can spread between pets and humans, though cross-transmissions occur at a lower rate than interspecies trasmission.

So far, the coronavirus has a mortality rate of ~1-2%, but may be higher for different people due to varying conditions. Infants and elders are especially susceptible to the Coronavirus.

Effects on the World

The largest number of confirmed cases are present in the United States, Italy, Spain, and France. As many as 213 countries and territories have registered the COVID-19 cases. The disease seems to spread at an exponential rate. The intiial discovery of Covid-19 in Italy on Januray 31, 2020, led to 7,350 confirmed cases and 360 deaths by March 8. Social distancing was emplaced, requiring at least 6 feet of distance between individuals.

Stock markets such as Nikkei, DowJones, and FTSE 100 saw their biggest quarterly drops in the first three months of the year since 1987.



According to the IMF, the unemployment rate has peaked at 10.3%. As a result, there are manufacturing crises in many countries where manufacturing decreased by 55% for pharmacies and 94% for motor vehicles. Educational systems worldwide have announced the closures of schools and universities, affecting 1.5 billion students worldwide and accounting for 87% of enrolled learners.

What You Can Do


  1. Respect social distancing, stay at least 6 feet away from others when in public.

  2. Wash your hands when you return from the outdoors. Wash and throughyl scrub with soap for at least 20-30 seconds.

  3. Because supplies during quarantine period are delivered to your doorstep, disinfect packages in a safe area with disinfectant spray.

  4. Minimize the amount of time spent outdoors.

  5. If you do go outdoors, remember to wear a mask and social distance.
